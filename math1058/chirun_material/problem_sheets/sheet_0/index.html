Problem 1 (Diet problem, reprise)

Let us consider an extension of the diet problem seen in the lectures to the case with a limited food availability.

Let be the set of nutrients, the set of of foods, the amount (in grams) of nutrient in a unit of food , for all , the least amount (in grams) of nutrient the diet should contain, the availability (in units) of food of type , and its cost (in pounds per unit).

Also assume that:

  • the amount (in units) of food should be no more than 50% of the total (amount of units).

  • the amount (in units) of food should be at least two times the total amount of all foods except for foods 1, 2, and 3.

Propose a linear programming formulation for this problem (where the total cost should be minimized).
