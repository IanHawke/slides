The PhD viva

  • Ian Hawke

Notes for the Maths and Mingle talk.

The event

Four people in the room:

  • You;
  • External examiner;
  • Internal examiner;
  • Independent chair.

Neither a celebration or an interrogation.

Builds on the thesis to check capability as an independent researcher.

As candidate you can ask for one of your supervisory team to be present in the room. They are not allowed to talk or contribute in any way. Hands up anyone who believes their supervisor is capable of this over a two hour discussion of research.

Recommendation

The examiners will recommend one of

  1. Award PhD;
  2. Award after minor changes (3 months, no new research);
  3. Award after modest changes (9 months, limited further research);
  4. Resubmit, lower degree, or fail.

Typically the recommendation is based on the thesis. Viva may shift recommendation up or down one.

Question

What have you done in your PhD?

We're after a brief summary here, less than one minute.

First tickbox criteria

The creation and interpretation of new knowledge through original research or other advanced scholarship, of a quality to satisfy peer review, extend the forefront of the discipline and merit publication.
Get your contribution in early.

Question

What's your field?

We're after the context: where does your research sit, who is interested in your contribution.

Second tickbox criteria

A systematic acquisition and understanding of a substantial body of knowledge which is at the forefront of an academic discipline or an area of professional practice.
Get your contribution in early.

Question

How did you do it?

Are you on top of the techniques that you used? Do you understand the implications, uses, problems and advantages of the methods? This typically won't be a line-by-line questioning of the text, instead it will (start) about the generalities, but applied to your specific case.

Third tickbox criteria

A detailed understanding of applicable techniques for research and advanced academic enquiry.
This is the closest to digging in to the details of the project. Be prepared to argue that your methods are not the best in all cases, but are the most suitable to your problem.

Question

What would you change?

Four years later, the field is four years smarter, and so are you. So, if you had a time machine then what would you change?

Fourth tickbox criteria

The general ability to conceptualise, design and implement a project for the generation of new knowledge, applications or understanding at the forefront of the discipline, and to adjust the project design in the light of unforeseen problems.
This is all about planning and your ability to see the big picture.

Summary

The examiners have to comment on

  • organisation
  • structure
  • presentation
  • authenticity
  • content
  • publishable quality
  • critical awareness of the subject.

Make it easy for the examiners to say yes.
