Numerical PDEs

Nonlinear PDEs are essential:

  • Fluids, gases, Navier-Stokes;
  • Solids, structures, Navier-Cauchy;
  • Gravity (Einstein), Finance (Black-Scholes), etc.

Numerical methods often most practical.

  • Approximate is "good enough";
  • Flexible to small change in model;
  • Many interesting mathematical issues.

Toy models

  1. Wave equation $$ \partial_{tt} \phi = c^2 \nabla^2 \phi; $$
  2. Advection $$ \partial_t \phi + \nabla_k \left( \phi v^k \right) = 0; $$
  3. Balance laws $$ \partial_t q + \nabla_k f^{(k)}(q) = s(q); $$
  4. Relaxation $$ \dot{q} = \tau^{-1} s(q). $$
